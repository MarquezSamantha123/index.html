Cat Breeds
Image Breed Description
British Shorthair The purebred version of the common domestic cat in Britain, characterized by a stocky build, a thick coat, and a large face. The "British Blue," which has an all-grey-blue coat, orange eyes, and a medium-sized tail, is the most well-known color variation.
Scottish Fold A domestic cat breed with a naturally occurring, dominant gene mutation that affects cartilage throughout the body and causes the ears to "fold," folding forward and downward toward the front of the skull, giving the cat a look that is frequently referred to as "owl-like."
Siberian Cat An ancient Russian domestic cat landrace that has only recently been recognized as a legitimate breed with international criteria since the late 1980s. Siberians range in size from medium to medium-large.
